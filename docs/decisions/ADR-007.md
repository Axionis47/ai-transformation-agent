# ADR-007: Victory Data Schema and RAG Seed Strategy

Status: Approved
Date: 2026-03-15
Decider: PM (Sai direction)

---

## Context

The RAG store was seeded with 10 generic industry case studies (seeds.json) that had
minimal structure: id, text, industry, company_type, use_case. These seeds produced
citations like "Based on a similar logistics company" without specific metrics, timelines,
or engagement details. The Consultant agent could not produce CFO-credible citations.

Sai identified this as a fundamental limitation: the RAG store should contain real
delivery wins that get better with every engagement. The system needs a competitive moat.

---

## Decision

Replace the flat seed schema with a structured Victory Data Schema (v1.0).

Key decisions made:

1. **Dedicated embed_text field** — only this field is embedded, not the full document.
   Embed_text is a structured concatenation of problem, solution, approach, and results
   in natural language. This prevents embedding noise from field names and metadata.

2. **Metadata fields for filtering, not embedding** — industry, size_label,
   maturity_at_engagement, duration_months go into ChromaDB metadata for filter-based
   retrieval. They are not embedded.

3. **Quantified results are required** — every victory record must have a primary_metric
   with a specific value, baseline, and outcome. Vague records are rejected at ingest.

4. **victories.json replaces seeds.json as primary RAG source** — seeds.json is retained
   as a fallback but is no longer the primary data source.

5. **20 simulated victories cover 10 industry verticals** — sized to provide meaningful
   retrieval coverage while remaining realistic in scope for a consulting firm.

---

## Consequences

Positive:
- Consultant agent can cite specific metrics, timelines, and engagement profiles
- CFO-credible citations: "We implemented X for a company your size and saw Y in Z months"
- System improves with every Tenex engagement (flywheel effect)
- Retrieval is more precise: problem-based similarity matching, not just outcome similarity

Negative:
- Schema is more complex — ingestion validation adds maintenance overhead
- embed_text must be regenerated if problem_statement or results fields change
- Seeds.json fixture tests may need updating (Backend ticket required)
